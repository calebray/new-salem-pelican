Mary Walcott (Upham map # 150)

The daughter of Jonathan and Mary (Sibley), Walcott was born in 1675. In 1692 the 17-year-old Mary lived with her father and his second wife, Deliverance (Putnam), in a homestead a short distance northwesterly from the parsonage and near the village training field. Her father was a yeoman and captain of the village militia and her stepmother was an aunt of Ann Putnam, Jr. Nathaniel Ingersoll was Mary¹s great uncle. (Richard Trask, The Devil Hath Been Raised, 1997, pp. 130-131.)
