# (Examination of [Sarah Cole](/tag/colsar.html) )

[October 3, 1692 ]

The Examination of [Sarah Cole](/tag/colsar.html) of Lynne Octob'r -- 3 -- 1692 

She saith that the same night [Capt Osgoods](/tag/osgjoh.html) wife was examined She  saw [Eliz: Colston](/tag/coleli.html) & [Abraham Coles](/tag/colabr.html) wife come into her house person-  ally to her apprehension and [Jno Wilkinsons](/tag/wiljoh2.html) wife of Malden & one  of her sisters & a little Girle she did not know; about 10 years old  one of them had a piece of board w'th. nails in it thro the board at  the end about a foot long as broad as her hand, That one of her  children was sorely afflected at that time, and s'd one of them did  strike her on the head w'th s'd board. They seemed to turn side  ways and so were gone w'ch was about midnight -- The child was  afflicted till [Abr. Coles](/tag/colabr.html) wife was take up. The beginning of the af-  fliction in our family was upon a fast Day about a month agoe [Abrah. Coles](/tag/colabr.html) wife was at my house She Commended my children much for   pretty children & they w'r both taken sick my boy & girle, the  Girle s'd she saw [A. Coles](/tag/colabr.html) wife afflict her severall times, had pins  thrust into her, was bit & scratched had a blow on her nose w'ch  caused her [to] nose to run Down w'th blood the last fit my child  had and Complained of her Aunt Cole was when s'd [A. Coles](/tag/colabr.html) wife  was brought to Salem

One night being in bed I was sorely afflicted and saw a ball of fire I  arose to see w't was the matter before I got a light it went away --  the last thing I saw was a Dog w'ch I went to strike w'th a spade and  was beat down my self this was about a week ago The Dog went out  at a crack: in the side of the house --

[Mary Warren](/tag/warmar.html) being afflicted was brought to [Goody Coale](/tag/colsar.html) & w'th her  touch was recovered. & looked on her twice & struck her downe  & recovered her w'th her hand [Mary Warren](/tag/warmar.html) said that she had sen this  [Coale](/tag/colsar.html) many times with [Goody Hart](/tag/hareli.html) & another wo. & that she had  not afflicted her till this night; & said that a woman that said her  name was baites & a Child both stoad up before her & beged for  Vengance, [Coale](/tag/colsar.html) owned that she & some others toyed w'th a Venus  glase & an Egg. what trade their sweet harts should be of
3[rd] Oct'r 1692  before  [*John Higginson](/tag/higjoh.html) Just. Peace ( Suffolk Court Files, Docket 2712, Page 49, Massachusetts Supreme Judicial Court, Judicial Archives, Massachusetts State Archives. Boston, MA. )</div><div markdown class="doc" id="n34.5"># Document: n34.5

# ( Statement of [John Cole](/tag/coljoh.html) v. [Sarah Cole](/tag/colsar.html) )

[October 3, 1692 ]

[Jno Cole](/tag/coljoh.html) Saith that Coming home to his house on saturday night  after his wife had seen strange sights -- That from that time his  house hath been troubled w'th cats & Dogs w'ch he saw often  running up & down & one night he thinks he saw a ball of fire,  & last Munday night being a week ago he saith as he was at prayer in  his family I heard something like a great thing flung against the  house & on a sudden it was at him & struck him on the head & on  one of his sides, and almost beat the breath out of his body -- so  that I was forced to break of prayer for about half a quarter of an  hour w'n he revived again he proceeded in his prayer his wife being  asked affirms she knew nothing of his being hindered in his prayer --  W'th in a week or 8 Days after S'd Cole saw a great Cat of an un-  usuall bignes at my door, staring me in the face I pursued it w'ch  went into the stalks near the house and tho it was very calm all  the stalks did wave as if there had been a strong wind -- and he  thinks since this some of his children have been afflicted by witch-  craft and s'd [Cole](/tag/coljoh.html) saith that for this 3 nights he had not lodged in  his own house being so affrighted that he was afraid to stay or  lodge in it being sorely molested always about the dead of the   night & was sorely handled last Saturday in his head & belly as if  a string had been twisted about his head
3 Oct'r 1692  Sworne in Court  atest. [*John Higginson](/tag/higjoh.html)  Justice peace ( Suffolk Court Files, Vol. 32, docket 2712 p. 49. )</div><div markdown class="doc" id="n34.12"># Document: n34.12
