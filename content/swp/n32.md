# (Warrant for apprehension of [Rachel Clinton](/tag/clerac.html), and Summons for Witnesses v. Rachel Clinton, and Officerâ€™s Return ) 

[March 29, 1692]

To the Constable of Ipswich -- 

Whereas There is Complaint Exhibbitted to the Honored Court now  holden at Ipswich -- In Behalfe of their majesties. against [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) -- Formerly the Wife of  [Lawrence Clenton](/tag/clelaw.html) of Ipswich on grounded Suspision of witchcraft,  & whereas Recognizance is Enterd, for prosecution --

You are hereby Required in their Majesties names forthwith or as  soon as may be to apprehend seize & bringe before the Honored  Court to be holden at Ipswich the sayd [Rachell Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) on the next  morrow morning at Eight a Clock In order to an orderly Examina-  tion, & Conviction & hereof fail not at Your perrill & for so doing  this shall be your warrant of which you are to make a true returne  as the Law derects:
Ipswich  March 29'th 1692)   P Curiam [*Tho's Wade](/tag/wadtho.html) Cler

To the Constable of Ipswich 

You are hereby required In their Majesties names to Sumons warne  & Require to appeare at the Court to be holden at Ipswich on the  morrow morning Viz't [Mary Fuller sen'r](/tag/fulmar1.html) & [Mary fuller Junior](/tag/fulmar2.html)  & [Allexsander Thomson jur](/tag/thoale.html) & [Richard fitts](/tag/fitric.html) & [Doct: John Bridgham](/tag/bridoc.html)  & [Thomas Maning](/tag/mantho.html) & [Nathaniel Burnam](/tag/burnat.html) all of Ipswich & [Thomas Knowlton jur](/tag/knotho.html) & [Mary Thorne](/tag/thomar.html) To Give in their severall Evidences be-  fore the Court to Cleare up the Grounds of Suspition of [Rachell Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) Being a Witch & hereof faile not at Your perrill but make  a true returne under Your hand as the Law Directs --
P. Curiam [*Thos Wade](/tag/wadtho.html) Cler. I have served this warrant and read it to [Rechell Clinton:](/tag/clerac.html) this morning : and sezed hur Body: and Left hur in the hands of [Samuell Ordeway:](/tag/ordsam.html) [to] have in the Court house Against your honoures shall  Call for hur and I have Red the severeall warrants one the other  sid written this morning save only [Richard fitts](/tag/fitric.html) and [Mary Thorne](/tag/thomar.html)  and [Richard fitts](/tag/fitric.html) I could not find and [Mary Thorne](/tag/thomar.html) is not well: as  witness my hand [*Joseph Fuller](/tag/fuljos.html) ,  Constable of Ipswich: Dated this 29'th March 1692  ( Suffolk Court Files, vol. 31, docket 2660, p. 140.)

# (Summons for Witnesses to the Examination of [Rachel Clinton](/tag/clerac.html))

[April 21, 1692 ]

To Sarje't [John Choate](/tag/chojoh.html), sen'r. To [Jonas Gregory](/tag/gregjon.html), To [James Burnam](/tag/burjam.html), all of Ipswich, [Mary Andrews](/tag/andmar.html), [Sarah Rogors](/tag/rogsar.html), [Marguriet Lord](/tag/lormar.html), [Sary Halwell](/tag/halsar.html) you & each of you are hereby Required in thair majesties names To make Your personall apperance before ye Worshipfull Maj'r [Sam'll Appleton](/tag/appsam.html) Esq., & ye Clerk of ye Court to be at ye house of Mr. [John Spark](/tag/spajoh.html) in Ipswich on ye 22d Day of This Instant aprile, at two o'clock afternoon. Then and There to Give in Your severall respective Evidences in behalf of [Rachell Clentons](/tag/clerac.html) being a witch, who is Then and Thair to be upon further Examination. Therefore So make Your apperance according to this Sumons fail nott at your perril.

Ipswich,  Dated aprill 21st, 1692 Curiam [Tho's Wade](/tag/wadtho.html), Clerk.

Ye Constable of Ipswich is alike Required to Give notis to ye said person, & to make returne as ye Law Directs. Curr [T. W.](/tag/wadtho.html)Clk.

According to this within written I haue Sumonsed and warned them: to Apere According to Time & Place by me [William Baker](/tag/bakwil.html), Constable.

Dated this 22d of april, 1692.

Thomas Franklin Waters, Ipswich in the Massachusetts Bay Colony (Ipswich, MA: Ipswich Historical Society, 1905):461.

# ( Deposition of [Mary Fuller, Sr.](/tag/fulmar1.html) , v. [Rachel Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) ) [+ March 30, 1692 ]

The Deposition of [Mary fuller sen'or](/tag/fulmar1.html) Aged About 41 years Saieth  About the 23 or fouorth of Last March: 1691/2 About Tenn of the  Clock [Rachell Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) cam to our house and Charged me with  Raisen Lies of hur About my Daughter and [Mary Thorne](/tag/thomar.html) and whille  she S'd [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) was drawing: my Brother [Joseph Fuller's](/tag/fuljos.html) boy Com in  and said their Betty was fall Downe Ded and this was as shee S'd  [Rechell](/tag/clerac.html) pased by: hur: A comming to our house and further Run up  to my Brothers [Joseph Fullers](/tag/fuljos.html) house for the space of Three hours  with out any motission [motion] of Life. #[deponent testifief] spake I Get hur hold up hur hand  If S'd [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) was the caus of it and shee ded: and when shee could  Speke shee S'd The womman with A white cape pased by and  struck hur: on the forehead:

( Suffolk Court Files, vol. 31, docket 2660, p. 140. )

# ( Deposition of [Thomas Boarman](/tag/boatho.html) v. [Rachel Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) )

[+ April 4, 1692 ]

This Deposition of [Thomas Boarman](/tag/boatho.html) sen'or aged 47 This Deponent  testifieth and saith that som wimen of worth and quality: Desired  me To Aquaint the seven men that [Rachell Clinton](/tag/clerac.html) was a great Diss-  turber unto them in the house in hunching them with hur Elboo: as  they went by hur s'd [Clinton](/tag/clerac.html) , and then the same Day ther I the  s'd [Boarman](/tag/boatho.html) : Desired the seven men to take som Cear the [Rachell Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) : might be for warned: not to com in to Thoes wimens house,  no more to Dissturbe them, and as I the s'd [Boarman](/tag/boatho.html) was Riding  whom: that night after I had bin, Before me Like a Cat as I Apper-  prehended: and then I looked wistfully upon it; and it Semed to be  sumething Like a Littell Doge: and then I pursued it: and it Kept  the same Distance in the parth before me: all though I Road heard  after it I could not over take it, then I looked once [to] my right  hand: and I saw a Grat Turkle that moved as fast as I Roud a Long:  and Then I thought of [Rachell Clinton](/tag/clerac.html) then the Littell Creature and  the Turkle vanished away: and further saith not.

( Suffolk Court Files, vol. 31, docket 2660, p. 140. )

# ( Deposition [William Baker](/tag/bakwil.html) v. [Rachel Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) )

[+ April 4, 1692 ]

The Deposion of [William Baker](/tag/bakwil.html) Aged 36 years Saith: About 10 years  Agoo: I living with my Master Rust: There was a Barrill of strong  beer brewed & the Day it was Brewed [Rachell Clinton](/tag/clerac.html) came there,  & was with sum small Thing:  But what it was I can not tell: & the Day  morning the Bere was put in to the Berrill: & that day said [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html)  went bakwords and forrowords, 6 or 7 times: up & Dow the lane  that Leds to our house & Did not com in to the house that Day:  & that night following: [hannah Rust:](/tag/rushan.html) went Downe to see whether the  Bere worked or no: & Puld out the tape & could find no Bere in  the Barrel: neither was there any appearance of wet upon the flouer:  and presently after we fild the Barril with worter and it was in a  night and Day and Did not Leke any of it out as we could see And  after that we our folke Brued another Barrel of Bere and put it in  the sam Barrill & it Did not Leke tall after that And further Saieth  not:

( Suffolk Court Records, vol. 31 Docket No. 2660, p. 140 )

# ( Testimony of [Mary Edwards](/tag/edwmar.html) v. [Rachel Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) )

[+ April 4, 1692 ]

Ipswich April the 4'th 1692 

The testimony of [Mary Edwards](/tag/edwmar.html) ageed about 52 yeres testifieth  & Saith that abought the 27'th day: of: this Last Dissember 1691  that  [Rachell CLenton](/tag/clerac.html) came to our houes & was very importinat with me  the s'd [Mary:](/tag/edwmar.html) to have Rome in our house to kepe share but I tould:  har the s'd [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) that there were no wages: provided to acommedat  har tharfore she -- must Rest har seat contented:)& at: this time: I  the s'd [Mary](/tag/edwmar.html) was mackeing of BLood pudens: and she the above  s'd [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) siting by the fier) by: her discorse had a great dissire to  have sum of that pudens: saying that it was very good foode:  & that she loved it very: well: so I the s'd [mary](/tag/edwmar.html) gave: her: the s'd  [Rachell](/tag/clerac.html) one of the pudens: the which she Reseaved vary: sCornfully:  & quickly after rose up: out of her seate & went a way: muttering  but what she said I coulld not tell: but about the Latter end of Jin-  nywary: the s'd [Edwards](/tag/edwmar.html) : had nine pigges: that ware a bout eight  weckes oulld that ware: tacken suddenly: five of them: &: died  & a bout a fortnight after that the s'd [Edwards](/tag/edwmar.html) : had thre: yerelings  tacken sudently that semed to be very harty: & was: in good: Case:  but sudenly was tacken with unusuall fittes: Jumping & Roreing till  thay tumbled downe with in a Littell tim after one a nother) as: to:  the Latter part of this testymony Releating to the death of my pigges:  & yearings I can attest two
[John s edwards, sen'r](/tag/edwjoh.html) his    mark:: & furthermore [mary: edwards:](/tag/edwmar.html) heard sum of her Children aske the s'd  [Reachell](/tag/clerac.html) how har hands ceame so: be Scracht: & swelled so & she:  made: This answar & said that she: had two or thre: Roges: catts:  that when she put downe her hand would scratch it: ( MS Ch K 1.40, vol. 2, p. 60, Dept. of Rare Books and Manuscripts )

# ( [James Fuller, Jr.](/tag/fuljam.html) , v. [Rachel Clenton](/tag/clerac.html) )

[++ April 4, 1692 ]

The deposition of [Jeams Fuller Junier](/tag/fuljam.html) aged about eighteen years  saith that on the 15'th Day of march Last: 1692 or there a bouts:  as to the Day: A bout 9 or 10: of the Clock: I was talking with [Good- man Perry](/tag/perrym.html) & telling him how the s'd [mary Fuller](/tag/fulmar2.html) my sister & [Mary Thorn](/tag/thomar.html) wer taken last night: & in the mene while, whilst he and I was  talking [Rachell Clinton](/tag/clerac.html) Cam in to our house & sat Downe by the fier  I asked hur what shee came bother for at this time of night, & shee  said shee Came to see what Lies them were that we raisd of hur  & I tould hur I Ded not know as I had Raisd Eny of hur: Presently  my onkles Boy Cam in & told me that ther Betty was Ded, and as  soon as Ever she s'd [Rachall](/tag/clerac.html) Herd them words: she run out of Dors:  and I followed hur as herd as I could & when I Came to the Dore I  could see nothing of hur: & it was a very Cler mone light night & I  Run up to my onkles: [Joseph fullers](/tag/fuljos.html) House: to see what the matter  was: & ther I found the Gerle Dede as the boys told me; & s'd onkl  fuller & I took hur up & Carried hur in the house & so shee conti-  nued for the space of 3 or 4 hours: before ther was any Apperance  of Life in hur; & the next Day I asked hur whoe mad hur so Last  night & she told me: She See Sumthing stare up at the Corner of  the [Stope] & Shee went a little way towards it & it Loced So Basly  she turned A bout to Run a way from it & it followed her & Knocked  hur Down

( MS Ch K 1.40, vol. 2, p. 68, Boston Public Library, Dept. of Rare Books and Manuscripts )

# (Deposition of [Thomas Burnam Jr.](/tag/burtho.html) v. [Rachel Clinton](/tag/clerac.html))

[+ March 30, 1692 ]

The Depsition of [Thomas Burnam](/tag/burtho.html) junr aged: 48: years who testifieth & saith yt som years sinc one sumer one of my Cows was very often milked & som times tow of them in my yard by my house: & thinking to Cach ye milker: I took paines & watched & one with me & thos nights yt I watched my Cowes were not milked & I arose one night a Litele before day: & stood in my indien Corn near whare my Cowes Lay & sone I saw a female stand in ye midele of ye yeard. whar ye cowes ware which by hear atier I thought was [Rachell Hatfell ^Clenton^](/tag/clerac.html) which as I thought uanished  a way: & a nother night I a rose before day & walked in ye street & just one ye breaking of day Came sudingly to my yeard wheare my Cowes Lay & that Cow that was most comonly milked stood & a parson a milking which presenly glanced from ye cow in ye Lickenesse of a gray Cat & run up ye back side of my house Scraching upon ye Shingells a bought fourty foot: & so ouer ye top of my house & further

saith not=

(in a different hand) Except yt ye Spring following the same Cow was found dead on ye Common, not mired nor Cast. nor throw poverty, or any Disease yt we know of.

Collection 77, Box 1, Folder 19, Maine Historical Society, Portland, ME.
