# (Warrant for apprehension of [Wilmott Reed](/tag/reewil.html) )
To the Constables  of Marblehead 
You are in theire Majest's names hereby required to apprehend and bring before Us [willmut Reed](/tag/reewil.html) the wife of [Samuell Reed](/tag/reesam.html) of Marblehead, on Tuesday next being the 31 day of this Instant moneth of May aboute ten of the Clock in the forenoon at the house of L't [Nathan'l Ingerslls](/tag/ingnat.html) in Salem Village; who Stands Charged with having Committed Sundry acts of Witchcraft on the bodys of [Mary Walcot](/tag/walmar.html) and [Marcy Lewis](/tag/lewmer.html) and Others[?] of Salem Village to theire great hurt &c, in order to her Examination Relateing to the aboves'd premises and hereof you are not to faile
Dated Salem  May 28'th 1692  P us [*John Hathorne](/tag/hawjoh.html) ] Assis'ts [*Jonathan. Corwin](/tag/corjoh.html) ] Assis'ts (Reverse) In answer to the withinmentioned warrant I have apprehended [Willmot Reed](/tag/reewil.html) Wife to [Sam'll Reed](/tag/reesam.html) of Marblehead & brought her to the house of [L't Ingersals](/tag/ingnat.html) , May the 31'th 92hi>. # (Examination of [Wilmott Reed](/tag/reewil.html) )

The examination of [Wilmot Redd.](/tag/reewil.html) Wife of [Samll Red](/tag/reesam.html) of marblehed fisherman. 31. May. 1692 

When this Examinant was brought in [Mercy Lewes](/tag/lewmer.html) [Mary Walcot](/tag/walmar.html) & [Abigail Williams](/tag/wilabi.html) fell into fits

[Mercy Lewes](/tag/lewmer.html) said this Woman hath Pincht me a great many times. [Mary Walcot](/tag/walmar.html) sais this Woman brought the Book to her.

[Ann Putman jun'r](/tag/putann2.html) saith she never hurt her, but #(she hath seen) she hath seen her once upen [Mercy Lewes](/tag/lewmer.html) & once upon [Mary Walcot](/tag/walmar.html) the last fast day.

[Eliz: Hubbard](/tag/hubeli.html) said this Examinant had brought the book to her, & told her she would knock her in the head, if she would not write.

[Ann Putman](/tag/putann2.html) said she brought the Book to her just now

[Eliz: Booth](/tag/booeli.html) fell into a fit, & [Mary Walcot](/tag/walmar.html) & [Ann Putman](/tag/putann2.html) said it was this Woman afflicted her.

[Susan: Sheldon](/tag/shesus.html) was ordered to goe to the Examinant but was knock down before she came to her, & being so carryed to said [Redd](/tag/reewil.html) in a fit, was made well after said [Redd](/tag/reewil.html) had graspt her arm.

[Eliz: Hubbard](/tag/hubeli.html) dealt with after the same manner

This Examinant was bid by the Magistrates to Look upon [Eliz: Hubbard](/tag/hubeli.html), & upon the examinants casting her eye upon said [Hubbard](/tag/hubeli.html), she the said [Hubbard](/tag/hubeli.html) was knockt down.

 

[Abig: Williams](/tag/wilabi.html) & [John Indian](/tag/indjoh.html) being carried to the Examinant in a grevious fit were made Well by her grasping their arms.

This examinant being often urged what she thought these Persons ailed; would reply, I cannot tell. Then being askt if she did not think they were Bewitcht: she answered I cannot tell And being urged for her opinion in the case All she would say was: my opinion is they are in a sad condition.

(Reverse) Examination of [Willmott Redd](/tag/reewil.html)

( Essex County Court Archives, Salem -- Vol. 2 Page 4 )

Essex ss. W'm & Mary by the Grace of God. of England Scotland france & Ireland King & Queen defend'rs of the faith &c'a

Greeting 

Wee Comand you to Warne & give Notice unto the wife & daughter of [Thomas Dodd](/tag/dodtho.html) the Wife & daughter of [Thomas Ellis](/tag/elltho.html) [John Caley](/tag/caljoh.html) [David Shapley](/tag/shadav.html) Wife & daughter [John Chinn](/tag/chijoh1.html). [Marthah Beale](/tag/beamar.html), [Elias Henly jun'r](/tag/heneli.html) & wiffe. [Benjamin Gale](/tag/galben.html), [Joane Bubbee](/tag/bubjoa.html), [Charity Pittman](/tag/pitcha.html), & [Jacob Wormwood](/tag/worjac.html), That they & Every of them be and personaly appear at the Court of Oyer and Terminer holden at Salem to morrow at Eight of the Clock in the Morning #(to morrow) there to Testify the Truth to the best of your knowledge on Severall Indictments Exhibited against [Wilmot Redd](/tag/reewil.html) hereof Make return fail not dated in Salem Sep'r 13'th 1692. & in the fourth yeare of Our Reign:

[*Stephen Sewall](/tag/sewall.html) Cle [Wilmot Redd](/tag/reewil.html)

(Reverse)

P mee [*James Smith](/tag/smijam.html) Cons't in Marblehead

# ([Mary Walcott](/tag/walmar.html) v. [Wilmott Reed](/tag/reewil.html) )

The deposistion of [mary walcott](/tag/walmar.html) who testifieth and saith I was for a considerable time afflectid by a woman which tould me hir name was [Redd:](/tag/reewil.html) and that she came from marblehead but on the 31: may 1692 being the day of the Examination of [willmott Redd](/tag/reewil.html) then I saw that she was the very same woman that tould me hir name was [Redd:](/tag/reewil.html) and she did most dreadfully afflect and tormet me dureing the time of hir Examination. for if she did but look upon me she would strick me down or almost choak me: also on the day of hir Examination I saw [willmott Redd:](/tag/reewil.html) or hir Ap#(Eliz: Booth) [Ann putnam](/tag/putann2.html) and I beleve in my heart that [willmott Redd](/tag/reewil.html) is a wicth and that she has often affleted and tormented me & the aforesad persons by acts of wicthcraft.

# ( [Charity Pitman](/tag/pitcha.html) v. [Wilmott Reed](/tag/reewil.html) )

The Testimony of [Charity Pitman](/tag/pitcha.html) of Marblehead.

This deponent aged twenty nine years affirms, that about five years agoe, [M'rs Syms](/tag/simmsm.html) of the Towne having lost some linnen which she suspected [Martha Laurence](/tag/lawmar.html) the girle which then lived with [Wilmott Redd](/tag/reewil.html) had taken up, desired the deponent to goe with her to [Wilmott Redds](/tag/reewil.html) , and demanding the same, having many words about the same, [M'rs Syms](/tag/simmsm.html) told her, that if she would not deliver them she would go to Salem to [mr Hathorne](/tag/hawjoh.html), and gett a speciall warrant for her servant girle; upon which the s'd [Redd](/tag/reewil.html) told her in my hearing, that she wished that she might never mingere [urinate], nor cacare [defecate], if she did not goe, and some short time after the deponent observed that the s'd [M'rs Syms](/tag/simmsm.html) was taken with the distemper of the dry Belly=ake, and so continued many moneths during her stay in the Towne, and was not cured whilst she tarryed in the Countrey, --
